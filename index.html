Academics & Research

Research Projects

Spent Coffee Grounds (SCGs) in Biopolymers

Student Researcher (RPI)

Enhanced methods to treat SCGs using chemical modifications and grafting techniques. Improved experimental procedure and established standardized drying protocols. Conducted characterization using Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA), executed tensile, three-point bend and shear testing, as well as XRD to assess crystallinity and mechanical performance.

Hemp Fiber Composites

Student Researcher (RPI)

Conducted a comprehensive literature review on commercial bio-composites to inform the design of new experimental methods aimed at investigating fiber architecture effects on mechanical properties. Overcame critical processing challenges by developing and incorporating a fold-mixing method, which significantly reduced air bubble defects in the cured composite matrix. Established a repeatable fabrication and testing protocol to evaluate tensile and flexural performance under challenging loading conditions.

Featured Projects

RPI Senior Capstone Design: Thermal Degradation of Silicone Gasket and Adhesion to Aluminum

  • Led characterization testing for an Eaton-sponsored gasket degradation study, investigating silicone–aluminum adhesion under accelerated thermal aging conditions.
  • Performed polymer characterization using GPC and GC-MS to identify low molecular weight siloxane species linked to gasket residue formation and increased opening force.
  • Developed an improved residue collection method for trace oil recovery, increasing reliability of experimental data compared to standard pipetting techniques.
  • Analyzed mass loss and molecular weight distribution data to support degradation modeling and guide design decisions for reducing gasket adhesion.

Competitive Bladesmithing Team (Material Advantage)

  • Founded and led a multidisciplinary engineering team in the design, metallurgical fabrication, and characterization of "Excelsior," a custom Damascus steel "knork" for the 2026 TMS Material Advantage Bladesmithing Competition.

  • Forged a pattern-welded blade utilizing alternating layers of 1095 high-carbon and 15N20 nickel-alloy steels, optimizing traditional hand-forging techniques with precise thermal heat treatments to maximize structural integrity.
  • Conducted advanced materials characterization, including metallography, SEM, EDS, and microhardness testing to map the relationships between thermomechanical processing, microstructure, and mechanical performance.
  • Managed end-to-end project execution, technical documentation, and cross-functional collaboration between student members, academic faculty, and industrial manufacturing mentors.
