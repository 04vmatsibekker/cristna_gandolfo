GlobalFoundries: Advanced Lithography Engineering Intern Summer 2025.

At GlobalFoundries, I created a new metrology-driven strategy for scanning test wafers, allowing for earlier fault detection in production. I collaborated with cross-functional teams to integrate these measurements across various tools and processes, which helped us proactively address equipment and batch issues. By optimizing our monitoring systems, I was able to improve data quality and speed up detection times while keeping our high measurement standards intact.

SABIC Specialty Plastics Technology: R&D Intern May 2024-December 2024

At SABIC, I explored the intersection of high-performance aerospace thermoplastics and large-format additive manufacturing (LFAM). I designed experiments to optimize critical production variables such as layer timing and temperature, and used advanced microscopy and mechanical testing to validate the results. It was a highlight to see my data analysis on processing parameters adopted into official company technical data sheets.

I also contributed to automotive projects by designing adhesion protocols and identifying tooling improvements, such as mirror-polished cavities, that enhanced the quality and consistency of our manufacturing runs.

Additionally, I led long-term testing on novel resin formulations to measure their durability against UV light and mechanical stress. By collaborating closely with our formulation chemists and product teams, I ensured our testing data directly informed the development of resins built for harsh, real-world environments.

Spent Coffee Grounds (SCGs) in Biopolymers

Student Researcher (RPI)

Enhanced methods to treat SCGs using chemical modifications and grafting techniques. Improved experimental procedure and established standardized drying protocols. Conducted characterization using Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA), executed tensile, three-point bend and shear testing, as well as XRD to assess crystallinity and mechanical performance.

Hemp Fiber Composites

Student Researcher (RPI)

Conducted a comprehensive literature review on commercial bio-composites to inform the design of new experimental methods aimed at investigating fiber architecture effects on mechanical properties. Overcame critical processing challenges by developing and incorporating a fold-mixing method, which significantly reduced air bubble defects in the cured composite matrix. Established a repeatable fabrication and testing protocol to evaluate tensile and flexural performance under challenging loading conditions.

Featured Projects

RPI Senior Capstone Design: Thermal Degradation of Silicone Gasket and Adhesion to Aluminum

I spearheaded an industry sponsored investigation into gasket degradation, focusing on silicone-aluminum adhesion under thermal stress. Beyond executing characterization protocols like GPC and GC-MS to identify residue-forming species, I engineered a more reliable residue collection method that improved data accuracy compared to traditional techniques. My analysis bridged the gap between raw data and actionable engineering, providing key insights that drove design decisions to reduce gasket adhesion.

Competitive Bladesmithing Team (Material Advantage)

I founded and led a multidisciplinary team to design, forge, and characterize "Excelsior"—a custom Damascus steel "knork" created for the 2026 TMS Material Advantage Bladesmithing Competition. The project was an deep dive into the art and science of metallurgy. We forged our blade using alternating layers of 1095 high-carbon and 15N20 nickel-alloy steels, combining traditional hand-forging techniques with precise thermal heat treatments to ensure maximum durability. Beyond the forge, I led the technical characterization of our work, utilizing metallography, SEM, EDS, and microhardness testing to map the complex relationships between our thermomechanical processing and the blade's final performance. Managing this project from concept to competition required constant collaboration, balancing the needs of our student team with guidance from academic faculty and industrial mentors.
